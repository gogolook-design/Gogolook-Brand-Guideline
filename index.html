Chapter 01
Brand Core
Gogolook is a TrustTech company founded in 2012, committed to building a fraud-free digital environment and a reliable communication network.
Brand Identity

The Gogolook identity is a system of visual and verbal elements working together to create a consistent, trustworthy experience across all touchpoints. Every design decision reinforces reliability, security, and integrity.

Pillar
Trust
Every design decision reinforces reliability, security, and integrity.
Pillar
Innovation
Modern, technology-forward aesthetics signal cutting-edge capability.
Pillar
Community
Humanistic, approachable design reflects Gogolook's commitment to people.
Brand Principles

Our direction principles guide every design decision and communication:

Cool & Confident
Design should feel assured and professional without being cold or corporate.
Authentic
Real, genuine, and transparent in every visual and verbal expression.
Human-Centered
Design creates a trustworthy and empowering experience for real people.
Voice & Tone

Gogolook's voice is clear, relatable, and empowering. Tone should feel calm, supportive, and never judgmental. Use simple language, focus on one clear action, and avoid technical jargon and fear-based messaging.

Frame around confidence and community — never around fear. Help people act confidently. One action per message, human verbs, zero jargon.
ChannelConstraint
Push notification title≤ 30 characters
Push notification body≤ 140 characters, 1 emoji max
Social media headers≤ 8 words
Chapter 02
Logo
The Gogolook logo embraces a humanistic and approachable design with a custom rounded sans serif typeface, reflecting trust within a robust community.
Evolution

Since 2012, the Gogolook logo has evolved to reflect the company's growth from a Taipei startup to a global TrustTech leader. Each iteration strengthened trust and modernity.

2012
Vibrant orange, warm and energetic innovation.
2014
Professional black, precision and reliability.
2022
Bold blue, strengthened global identity.
2024
Modern rounded sans serif, trust and connectivity.
Wordmark

The wordmark has a single approved form and must always be displayed unaltered. There is no standalone icon — the wordmark is the logo. The bright blue represents cutting-edge technology, innovation, and modernity.

Color Usage

The wordmark should most often appear in Gogolook Blue and white only.

Preferred
Gogolook Blue on white, or white on Gogolook Blue. Use these in most contexts.
Optional · Monochrome
Black or white monochrome. For single-color print, embossed, frosted, or etched finishes.
Clear Space & Minimum Size

Clear space should be the height of the "G" in the wordmark on all four sides. No other elements should intrude on this zone.

Clear Space
Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "G" on all sides.
Minimum Size
Web: 100px wide / 27.2px tall. Print: 0.8 in (20mm) wide / 1.3 in (35.2mm) tall.
Incorrect Usage

It is important that the appearance of the logo remains consistent. Never alter the wordmark in any of these ways:

Do not scale disproportionately.
Do not use colors outside the core palette.
Do not reconstruct or distort the logo.
Do not add a drop shadow.
Do not tile the wordmark into a pattern.
Do not add gradients to the logo.
Co-Branding

When Gogolook partners with other brands, the Gogolook logo is positioned on the left, followed by the partner logo on the right, separated by a vertical divider. Gap between logos equals the width of the "G" in the Gogolook wordmark.

Chapter 05
Layout
A consistent 12-column grid and defined margins ensure order, hierarchy, and breathing room across all formats.
12-Column Grid

All layout tasks use a 12-column grid. The grid allows a page to be divided evenly into units of 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, or 12. Use as few columns as possible to avoid overly complex layouts.

Chapter 06
Photography
Gogolook photography is expressed through three key principles: Connected, Innovative Lifestyle, and Trustworthy.
Connected

Our aim is to showcase collaboration and connection. Gogolook unites people, highlighting harmony and unity. Photography should use warm mood tones and lighting to emphasize the sense of community.

Innovative Lifestyle

Relatable, human, warm. Our lifestyle photography should be bright and vibrant, with uncluttered backgrounds and a shallow depth of field. We use an editorial-style approach with minimal styling to showcase real people in genuine, candid moments.

Environment

Industry-specific imagery provides context and serves as a backdrop to our brand storytelling. The composition should be engaging and authentic, often incorporating abstract elements or close-up crops to create rich, textured backgrounds.

Do's & Don'ts
✓ Do
  • Use high saturation with warm, rich grading
  • Choose natural and candid selects
  • Ensure modern-day attire and environments
  • Combine people with graphics authentically
✕ Don't
  • Avoid unnaturally dark photos
  • Avoid clichéd or overly staged images
  • Avoid outdated work environments
  • Avoid graphics dominated by binary code
